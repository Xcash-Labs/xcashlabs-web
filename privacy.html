Privacy

XCash Klassic Web Wallet is a non-custodial wallet. We do not store your funds, seed phrase, or private spend key.

Short version: Your seed phrase and private spend key remain in your browser. The wallet server cannot spend your funds.

What We Do Not Collect

  • Seed phrases — never transmitted or stored by us.
  • Private spend keys — remain in your browser and are used locally to sign transactions.
  • Wallet passwords — never transmitted.
  • User accounts — there are no registrations or login accounts.
  • Marketing data — no newsletters, advertising profiles, or tracking systems.

What The Wallet Sends

Blockchain Queries

The wallet performs blockchain lookups to retrieve balances, transaction history, network status, and fee estimates.

Light Wallet Service

When using the XCash Klassic Light Wallet Server (LWS), your wallet address and private view key may be transmitted to the server so it can scan the blockchain for incoming transactions.

The server can see:
  • Your public wallet address
  • Your private view key
  • Signed transactions submitted for broadcast
The server cannot see:
  • Your seed phrase
  • Your private spend key
  • Your wallet password
  • Any information that would allow spending your funds

Hosting Infrastructure

wallet.xcashlabs.org is operated by XCash Labs. Like any website, standard web requests are processed to deliver the application and provide wallet functionality.

We do not intentionally collect or retain wallet secrets, private spend keys, or seed phrases.

Browser Storage

Wallet information may be temporarily stored in your browser session to allow the wallet to remain unlocked while you use it. Closing your browser session or clearing browser data may remove this information.

Third-Party Services

The wallet is designed to minimize reliance on third-party services. No analytics platforms, advertising networks, or tracking scripts are required for normal wallet operation.

Open Source

The XCash Klassic Web Wallet is open source. Users are encouraged to review the source code, audit the software, or self-host the wallet infrastructure.

Changes To This Policy

Any changes to this privacy policy will be reflected in the public source repository.
